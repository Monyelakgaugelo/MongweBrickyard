— Our Story

Since 1908, the same yard, the same fire.

Founded by Albert Holloway on six acres of river clay, our yard has fed the chimneys, façades and garden walls of three counties for over a century.

We still dig from the original seam. We still hand-press the soft-mud brick that built half the town's mills. And we still let every batch rest seven days before it sees the kiln.

"A brick should outlive the person who laid it. Twice over."

— Edward Holloway, 4th generation
